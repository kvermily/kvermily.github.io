The Cost of Living: A Working Autobiography

Deborah Levy

"Life falls apart. We try to get a grip and hold it together. And then we realize we don't want to hold it together."

What if the love Anne Elliot had chosen to given up wasn't Captain Wentworth but a life created for herself? Deborah Levy's 2018 memoir explores the traditional tropes and trappings of contemporary womanhood and sets about crafting a new narrative framework with a new type of heroine.

If you enjoy this, you'll want to read Levy's previous memoir installment, Things I Don't Want to Know, and her follow-up, Real Estate.
